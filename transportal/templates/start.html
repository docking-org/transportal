Welcome to the FDA Transporter Database page. The purpose of this database is to be a useful repository of information on transporters important in the drug discovery process as a part of the US Food and Drug Administration-led Critical Path Initiative. Information includes transporter expression, localization, substrates, inhibitors, and drug-drug interactions.
*New 2021* The updated Transportal now contains updated information on the in vitro and clinical substrates and inhibitors of 12 drug transporters as well as new information on expression levels (both transcript levels and protein levels) of the transporters in various tissues of pharmacologic importance. In addition, the TransPortal database now includes “TICBase”, an online repository of environmental chemical interactions with drug transporters from humans and other pharmacologically relevant mammals. TICBase lists 24 different SLC and ABC-type mammalian drug transporters interacting with 189 unique compounds, resulting in a total of 355 molecular interactions. Of those, 283 interactions are with human drug transporters, 36 with mouse, 15 with rat, and 21 interactions are reported with monkey drug transporters.
