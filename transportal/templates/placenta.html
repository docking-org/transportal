Notes:
  • The study of the maternal-fetal barrier is highly dynamic. As new information becomes available we will update this page.
  • The expression of transporters at this barrier has been shown to change throughout gestation (see Mikheev et al. Reprod Sci. 2008 Nov;15(9):866-77). The figure above represents the localization of transporters in a full-term placenta.
  • The arrows indicate the transporter's primary direction of transport. However, for some SLC transporters, transport in the opposite direction is also possible. For example, glutarate efflux is trans-stimulated by extracellular estrone-3-sulfate or para-aminohippurate in OAT4-expressing S2 cells (Ekaratanawong et al., J Pharmacol Sci. 2004 Mar;94(3):297-304).
  • The orange transporters OATP4A1, ATP7B, and ENT1 are shown, but not currently contained in our database.
